Classic Apple Crisp

Description

It's a classic for a reason.

This recipe is pulled from King Arthur Baking.

Ingredients

To make this deliciousness, you'll need:

Filling

  • 3 pounds apples
  • 1/4 cup (57g) rum, apple cider or juice (we say rum)
  • 1/4 to 3/4 cup (53-159g) light or dark brown sugar
  • 2 Tbsp (28g) butter, melted
  • 2 Tbsp (43g) boiled cider, optional but best to have it
  • 1 1/2 tspn apple pie spice, or 1 tspn cinnamon + 1/4 tspn nutmeg + 1/4 tspn giner
  • 3 Tbspn (20g) all purpose flour or tapioca flour
  • 1/4 tspn table salt

Topping

  • 3/4 cup (90g) all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup (45g) quick-cooking oats
  • heaping 1/4 Tbsp table salt
  • 2/3 cup (142g) light or dark brown sugar, packed
  • 1 tspn cinnamon
  • 3/4 tspn baking powder
  • 8 Tbsp (113g) unsalted butter, cold, but into pats
  • 1/2 cup (57g) diced pecans or walnuts, optional

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 350°F. Grease a 9" square pan or similar-size casserole pan.
  2. Slice the apples about 1/4" thick. Toss them with the remaining filling ingredients and spread evenly in the prepared pan.
  3. For the topping: whisk the flour, oats, sugar, cinnamon, and baking powder.
  4. Add the cold butter and work them into the crumbly mixture. Stir in the nuts if you're using them.
  5. Spread this mixture over top the apples in the pan.
  6. Set the pan on a parchment or foil-lined baking sheet to catch drips.
  7. Bake the crisp for about 60 minutes, until it's bubbling and the top is golden brown.
  8. Remove the crisp from the oven and let it cool for at least 20 minutes before serving. If you serve it while it's warm, it may be soft. If it's cooled completely, it'll be firm. Scoop some vanilla ice cream as added bonus.
